Gangster whose street name is 'Dangerous' murdered two people in shootout at Tacoma bar called The A

A fatal shooting at a Washington bar that led to three people being injured and two dead was due to an argument over one person's hairline, according to documents. 

The fight broke out at Tacoma's The Alleycat Patio and Lounge when a man identified as 'L.C' tried to insert himself into conversations with a group of women he didn't know. 

The suspect, Alante Peterson, intervened and allegedly told the man: 'You can just leave the girls alone.' 

A cell phone video shown to the police apparently shows several people arguing over someone's hairline. Soon after, people in the bar heard a gun slide back and the shooting began.

Peterson, who goes by the street name 'Dangerous,' began turning in a circle while firing the gun and started randomly shooting at people, according to witnesses quoted in court documents. 

He has been charged with two counts of first-degree aggravated murder, two counts of first-degree murder and three counts of assault for the three injured in the shooting.
