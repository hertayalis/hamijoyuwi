Don Shane: A Career of Dedication

Don Shane’s career was marked by his dedication to sports reporting and journalism. He embarked on his professional journey by calling plays for esteemed teams such as the Detroit Pistons, University of Michigan, and Michigan State University’s football and basketball teams. He further honed his skills at WBZ-TV in Boston and WMAQ-TV in Chicago before joining WDIV and ultimately finding a home at WXYZ-TV Channel 7.

A Legacy of Coverage

Don Shane’s remarkable career as a sportscaster saw him covering some of the most significant sporting events. He provided insights and commentary on the NBA Finals, Stanley Cup, Rose Bowl, Final Fours, World Series, and boxing matches featuring local fighters. His passion for sports journalism led him to Channel 7, where he also worked as a news reporter in the early 1980s.

Retirement and Last Years

Don Shane bid farewell to his illustrious career in broadcasting on March 21, 2012, surrounded by family and friends at Broadcast House. Following his retirement, he relocated to Arizona before spending his final years close to his daughters in California. The family welcomed their first grandchild in May 2022, marking a joyous milestone.
