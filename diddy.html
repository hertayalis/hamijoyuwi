Biography

Diddy was born in New York City, NY on November 4, 1969. Born Sean Combs but better known as Diddy, P. Diddy, or Puff Daddy, he is a Grammy Award-winning hip-hop mogul whose debut album, No Way Out, went 7x platinum, and whose song, “I’ll Be Missing You,” became a #1 Billboard hit. Forbes magazine ranked him the richest man in hip-hop in 2013. He signed and produced Mary J. Blige‘s 1994 record, My Life, which Rolling Stone ranked as one of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. He grew up in a public housing project, and his father was killed for being an associate of drug kingpin Frank Lucas.
