Bob Ross Biography; Age, Education, Career, Cause Of Death

Robert Norman Ross also known as Bob Ross was Bron in Daytona Beach, Florida, the United States of America on 29th October, 1942. He is arguably one of the best painters America and the world have ever seen. Bob Ross sadly died away on 4th July, 1995.

How Did Bob Ross Die?

Bob Ross was one of the most important figures in painting. His death really broke a lot of hearts and caused so much pain to most of his lovers and fans.

The actual cause of his demise was colitis/lymphoma. It is revealed that, throughout Bob Ross’ adult life, he had been critically sick. He also smoked lots of cigarettes. At the age of 52, Bob Ross was reported dead on 4th July, 1995 of colitis or lymphoma in Orlando, Florida.

According to stories by those who personally knew the painter, it seemed that, he knew was going to die at that early age. Bob Ross suffered two heart attacks and have also survived his initial battle with cancer. The second, which was identified as lymphoma, didn’t go so well for Bob.

His gravestone is beautifully situated in Woodland Memorial Park. It’s been marked ‘Television Artist’. His students, in particular, on most days leave some paintings at his resting place anytime they come for a visit.

After his death, most of his paintings were donated to charity and some to PBS Stations. Bob Ross continue to remain as one of the highest-paid and popular American painters in history.

Bob Ross’ Marriage

Bob Ross’ first marriage happened with his beautiful ex-wife, Vivian Ridge in 1965. Later, things escalated and they got divorced after 12 years of marriage in 1977. After Vivian Ridge, Bob moved on with life and tied the knot the same year with Jane Ross and she suddenly passed away in 1992. Then somewhere in 1995, Bob Ross’ path met with Lynda Brown. They got married and lived the best of life till after his unfortunate demise the same year. He had two children.

Education

Bob Ross was raised in Florida. After he had completed just one year at High School and worked with his father at a carpentry shop. He was dropped out of school in ninth grade at about 18. He was enlisted in the United States Air Force. He was then stationed in Alaska. During this course, Bob Ross headed forward in the early 1960s for his first-ever painting class at a United Service Organisation club.

Career

Bob Ross was an American artist(painter) and also a television star. He hit the world of fame with his PBS television show The Joy of Painting, which aired from 1983 to 1994. That show created lots of positive moves in his painting career.

Just after Bob Ross had returned from Air Force, he attended many art schools until he studied the wet-on-wet technique from William Alexander, who later became Bob’s rival. As generous and kind as Bob Ross was, he did not keep the knowledge to himself. He went on and taught his colleagues a d friends who were willing to learn. Then somewhere in the early 1980s, Bob was given his own television show based on the wet-on-wet painting technique.

Bob Ross battled with two heart attacks and survived his first battle with cancer. The second one, identified as lymphoma, didn’t go so well for Bob. His actual death is said to have been caused by lymphoma.

How Old Was Bob Ross?

The legendary painter died at the age of 53. He was born on 29th October, 1942 and died on 4th July, 1995.

Did Bob Ross Have A Son?

Yes. Bob Ross had a son whose name is Steve Ross. Steve Ross was birthed by Bob Ross’ first wife, Vivian Ridge.

Is Bob Ross Dead?

Yes. Bob Ross died from lymphoma on 4th July, 1995 exactly the same year he married his third wife, Lynda Brown. He was 53.
